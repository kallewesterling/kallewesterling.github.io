Announced: American Burlesque - Summer 2017

November 12, 2017

It was announced today that I will be teaching the course "The History of American Burlesque" at New York University this coming summer. I also received the promotional image that is attached to this blog post.

Here's the course description from Tisch's Drama program's website:

Coined in the 16th century as a literary or theatrical form that inverts form and content, burlesque is a subset of parody that either elevates the mundane or vulgarizes the lofty. This course will cover some of the major historical shifts in American burlesque traditions including Thompsonian burlesque (and those that followed), female minstrel shows, hootchy cootchie dance, burlesque wheels, the emergence of striptease, queens of burlesque, exotic dancing, and the neo-burlesque movement. Rather than codify the defining characteristics and time periods of these historical moments, we will seek to understand and trace how the definitions, conceptual preoccupations, and performance techniques of burlesque have adapted and changed over time. We will watch films that document burlesque; read biographies of major figures and scholarly work about burlesque, theatre, and popular culture; attend neo-burlesque performances, and discuss the neo-burlesque and performance art movements with guest artists.

I originally taught this class together with Dr. Lynn Sally but this semester I will teach the class by myself. Terrifying but I am very thankful to Dr. Sally for allowing me to take over this class.
